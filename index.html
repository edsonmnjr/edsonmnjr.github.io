Welcome! I am Edson Mendes Nunes Junior, Adjunct Professor in the Department of Political Studies at FLACSO Ecuador. I hold a PhD in Political Science from Fluminense Federal University (UFF), an MA and a BA in Political Science from the Federal University of the State of Rio de Janeiro (UNIRIO), and a BA in International Relations from IBMR University.

My research examines democratic resilience and institutional change in the Global South, with a particular focus on how disinformation, conspiracy theories, and platform governance challenge democratic institutions in Latin America and beyond. I have recently expanded this agenda to study the institutional and societal implications of artificial intelligence, including its use in political communication and its regulatory challenges for democratic governance.

My doctoral dissertation examined radical left political parties in Brazil and their adaptation to the democratic system in the early 21st century, combining qualitative and quantitative methods — electoral data analysis using R, document analysis with NVivo, and interviews with party members and affiliates. My master's thesis analyzed Latin American media coverage of the Constituent Assembly convened by Nicolás Maduro in Venezuela.

Beyond several book chapters, my work has been published in journals such as Revista Brasileira de Segurança Pública, Desigualdade & Diversidade, Passagens, and Revista Historiar, and discussed in public-facing outlets including The Conversation and Alma Preta. My current research interests include democratic erosion, digital politics and platform governance, political institutions in Latin America, and the political challenges posed by artificial intelligence.
